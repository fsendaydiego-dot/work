Preliminary verdict

Choose Plain if…

You're building a developer or community product, your customers use Slack/Teams/Discord, you have a small agent team but a large customer base, and you want bundled AI + deep product analytics. Requires dev capacity for custom channel builds.

Choose Help Scout if…

Email + WhatsApp + Facebook are your primary channels, you want the simplest setup with no-code Notion sync, and your agent team grows faster than your contact volume. Best for non-technical teams that want things working out of the box.

Key distinction: Plain ships three distinct AI layers — an autonomous customer-facing agent (Ari), an agent-assist copilot (Sidekick), and a codebase query tool (Lookup). Help Scout's AI is a single layer focused on reply drafting and FAQ deflection. Plain's architecture is meaningfully deeper.

Plain AI — best for

Teams that want an autonomous agent running 24/7 across all channels. Technical products where support agents need AI-assisted access to product context, conversation history, and code. Teams that want to plug in specialised third-party AI agents or build their own.

Help Scout AI — best for

Teams that want simple FAQ deflection before a ticket is created. Good enough for straightforward consumer support questions where the answer lives in a knowledge base article. Lower ceiling but lower complexity — no AI infrastructure to manage.

Key distinction: Plain is designed to feed insights back to your product team — it automatically surfaces themes, trends, and churn signals from support conversations. Help Scout's reporting is solid for support operations but data stays inside the tool unless exported. Neither has a native BI connector; both rely on API exports or middleware for deep analysis.

Plain reporting — best for

Teams that want support data to actively inform their product roadmap. Plain's auto-theme detection, churn signals, and daily digests are unique — no other platform in this comparison surfaces those insights without manual work. Best if analytics is a first-class requirement, not an afterthought.

Help Scout reporting — best for

Teams that need solid support operations metrics — response times, agent performance, CSAT — without complexity. Help Scout's reporting is clean and accessible to non-technical managers. Better CSAT tooling than Plain. If you need deeper analysis, export via Zapier to a BI tool.

Cost questions to pressure-test

1.What's your support agent headcount in 12 months? Plain's cost grows with agents — 5 agents on Horizon = $269 + 2×$89 = $447/mo.
2.How many unique customers will you support monthly? Help Scout charges per contact — 1,000 contacts/month can exceed $150–200+/mo before AI fees.
3.What's your expected AI deflection rate? Plain bundles 250 resolutions in Horizon. At 500 resolutions/month: Plain adds $250; Help Scout adds $375. At 1,000: roughly equal.
4.Do you plan to use middleware (Zapier/Make) for Notion or Telegram? Add $20–50+/mo for that tool depending on automation volume — applies to Help Scout more than Plain.

Channel requirements

1.Which channels are your customers actually on today vs where you expect them in 2 years? Channel strategy should drive platform choice. All
2.For Telegram: does your team have dev capacity to build a custom connector on Plain's API, or do you need a no-code path via Help Scout + Zapier? All
3.Do you need voice/phone support? Only Help Scout supports it natively. Plain has no voice capability. Help Scout only
4.Will customers reach you via WhatsApp, Facebook, or Instagram? Help Scout wins clearly — Plain has no native path for these. Help Scout

AI agent & automation

5.Do you need an autonomous AI agent that can fully resolve complex conversations end-to-end, or just FAQ deflection before a ticket is created? Plain's Ari does the former; Help Scout's AI Answers does the latter. Critical
6.Do your support agents need AI-assisted access to customer history and product context while writing replies? Plain's Sidekick is purpose-built for this; Help Scout's AI Drafts is more basic. Plain
7.Is technical support a big part of your tickets (e.g. bugs, API errors, account edge cases)? Plain's Lookup and its third-party AI agent marketplace (Duckie, Inkeep) are meaningfully better for this. Plain
8.What % of tickets do you expect AI to deflect per month? Model costs carefully — at 500 resolutions/month, Plain saves ~$125/mo vs Help Scout. At 1,000 they're roughly equal. All

Data, reporting & analytics

9.Do you want support data to actively inform your product roadmap? Plain's automated Themes is unique and doesn't exist in Help Scout — it requires zero manual effort to surface recurring issues. Plain leads
10.Do you need to push support data to a data warehouse (BigQuery, Redshift, Snowflake)? Both require an ETL layer, but Plain's API-first design makes building a pipeline cleaner. Help Scout can use Zapier → a warehouse connector. All
11.How important is CSAT measurement? Help Scout has stronger native CSAT tooling and it's available on all paid plans. Plain has CSAT but it's a secondary feature. Help Scout stronger
12.Do you need SLA reporting with breach alerts? Plain has this natively; Help Scout does not. If you have response time commitments to customers, this is a hard requirement. Plain only

Integrations & data repositories

13.Is Notion a destination (tickets logged there) or a source (agents pull knowledge from it)? The source pattern is only practical with Plain's API + Notion MCP. Plain
14.Do you use Linear or GitHub for bug tracking? Plain has native two-way integrations for both. Help Scout requires middleware. Plain stronger
15.Do you use Salesforce or HubSpot as your CRM? Both platforms have native connectors — no differentiation here. Equal

Cost, scale & fit

16.What's your support agent headcount in 12 months? Plain scales with agents; Help Scout scales with customers. Use the Cost tab to model both trajectories. All
17.How fast do you need to go live in-app? Help Scout's Beacon is drop-in ready in hours. Plain's headless portal gives full control but needs developer implementation time. All
18.How worried are you about vendor lock-in? Plain is API-first and provides migration tools. Help Scout's conversation history is harder to bulk-export at scale. Plain safer
19.Is your app global or multi-language? Neither platform has strong native multilingual AI. If this is important, factor in the additional tooling needed regardless of platform chosen. Gap for both
20.Have you run a 14-day trial on both platforms with real traffic? No comparison document replaces live testing. Both offer free trials — running real conversations through both will surface UX and integration friction that specs cannot. Strong recommendation
