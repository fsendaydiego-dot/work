No channel player operates at this layer. All are downstream consumers of LLM APIs. MASPs and Vertical AI firms engage most deeply via custom fine-tuning and enterprise API agreements.
LAYER 02
Speech & Voice Infra
Deepgram · ElevenLabs · LiveKit
AAA — Indirect via Vapi CCaaS — Direct integration MASP — Direct API usage
AAAs access speech infra indirectly through Vapi/Synthflow. CCaaS integrators and MASPs often connect directly to Deepgram/ElevenLabs APIs for custom pipelines.
LAYER 03A
Voice Agent Platforms
Vapi · Synthflow · Retell · Bland
🎯 AAA — Primary reseller Niche — Core build tool MASP — One tool of many Vertical AI — Occasionally
AAAs and Niche Automation firms are the dominant resellers of Vapi/Synthflow. MASPs may include it as one component of a broader stack. CCaaS integrators and BPOs rarely touch these platforms.
LAYER 03B
Enterprise CC Platforms
Genesys · NICE · Five9 · Talkdesk
🎯 CCaaS — Core domain MASP — Integration work BPO — Platform clients Vertical AI — Overlay
CCaaS Integrators are the dominant channel here. BPOs are often buyers/operators of these platforms. AAAs almost never touch enterprise CC platforms — different buyer, different deal size.
LAYER 03C
LLM Chatbots / AI Assistants
Intercom · Zendesk · ChatGPT
Niche — Chat automation builds MASP — Deployment & config Vertical AI — Domain chatbots AAA — Some bundle chat
MASPs, Niche firms, and Vertical AI consultants are the primary implementers here. AAAs occasionally bundle chat alongside voice but voice remains their primary focus.

03 · Player Deep Dives — Ecosystem Position, Buyers, Threat to AAAs
🏷️
Voice AI Agency Resellers (AAAs)
The reference player. SMB-focused, voice-first, platform-agnostic white-labelers.
Ecosystem Layer
Layer 4 · Distribution & Implementation Channel
Primary Platforms Used
  • Vapi, Synthflow, Retell AI, Bland AI
  • GoHighLevel (CRM bundling)
  • Make / Zapier (workflow automation)
Primary Buyers
  • SMB local businesses (1–50 employees)
  • Real estate, healthcare, home services, legal
  • Anyone with high call volume + small staff
Revenue Model
  • $500–$5,000 setup fees
  • $300–$2,000/month retainer or SaaS
  • Usage markup on platform costs
Competitive Moat
  • Speed of deployment (days not months)
  • SMB relationships + vertical knowledge
  • Low price point accessible to long tail
🔬
Vertical AI Consultants
Deep domain experts who build bespoke AI for one industry. Think "AI for Healthcare" or "AI for Legal."
Ecosystem Layer
Layer 4 · Specialist Implementation · sits above platforms, below end market
Primary Platforms Used
  • Custom LLM fine-tuning (OpenAI, Anthropic)
  • Nuance DAX, Suki, Hyro (healthcare)
  • Harvey AI, Clio (legal)
  • Rarely use Vapi/Synthflow
Primary Buyers
  • Hospital systems, large clinics
  • Am Law 200 law firms
  • Financial institutions
  • Mid-market to enterprise only
Revenue Model
  • $50K–$500K+ project engagements
  • Ongoing retainer for maintenance
  • Per-seat or outcome-based licensing
vs Agency Resellers
Vertical AI consultants play a completely different game — 10–100x the deal size, 10x longer sales cycle, completely different buyer. They rarely compete with AAAs. Potential partnership: AAAs could white-label vertical AI outputs for SMB versions of the same use case.
PARTNER POTENTIAL
⚙️
Managed AI Service Providers
Full-stack AI operations partners who own the entire AI infrastructure for mid-market and enterprise clients.
Ecosystem Layer
Layer 4 · Full-stack managed services · touches Layers 1–5
Primary Platforms Used
  • Full LLM API stack (all providers)
  • Voiceflow, Botpress, Rasa for orchestration
  • Deepgram, ElevenLabs direct
  • Azure AI, AWS AI, GCP AI services
Primary Buyers
  • Mid-market companies ($10M–$500M revenue)
  • Enterprise ops and IT leadership
  • Companies wanting "AI without hiring"
Revenue Model
  • $5,000–$50,000/month managed retainer
  • Implementation projects $25K–$250K
  • Outcome-based pricing emerging
vs Agency Resellers
MASPs target a buyer 10–50x larger than AAAs and deliver 10x more complex solutions. Low direct competition today, but MASPs are beginning to offer packaged SMB tiers — which could encroach on AAA territory by 2026–27.
LOW THREAT NOW WATCH 2026+
🎯
Niche Automation Firms
Hyper-focused automation specialists who own one workflow in one vertical extremely well.
Ecosystem Layer
Layer 4 · Specialized implementation · often productizing into SaaS
Primary Platforms Used
  • Vapi or Retell for voice components
  • Make / Zapier / n8n for workflow
  • Airtable, Notion for data layer
  • GoHighLevel for CRM in some cases
Primary Buyers
  • One specific vertical (e.g. HVAC only)
  • SMB to small mid-market
  • Often sold via industry associations
Revenue Model
  • $500–$3,000/month SaaS pricing
  • Moving toward productized services
  • Often bootstrapped, high-margin
vs Agency Resellers
Closest competitor to AAAs in the ecosystem. Both use the same platforms (Vapi, Synthflow), target similar buyer sizes, and charge comparable prices. Key difference: Niche firms go deep in one vertical vs AAAs going broad across many. Direct competitors for the same client in overlapping verticals.
HIGHEST DIRECT THREAT
📞
CCaaS Integrators
Certified partners of Genesys, NICE, Five9, and Talkdesk who implement and customize enterprise contact center platforms.
Ecosystem Layer
Layer 4 · Enterprise implementation channel · bridges Layers 3B and 5
Primary Platforms Used
  • Genesys Cloud, NICE CXone, Five9
  • Talkdesk, 8x8, Amazon Connect
  • Salesforce Service Cloud (CRM layer)
  • Never use Vapi/Synthflow
Primary Buyers
  • Enterprise (500+ seat contact centers)
  • VP/Director of Customer Experience
  • IT and Telephony leadership
Revenue Model
  • $100K–$2M+ implementation projects
  • Ongoing managed support contracts
  • Revenue share on platform licensing
vs Agency Resellers
Zero direct competition today — completely different buyer, platform, price point, and sales cycle. However, as enterprise CCaaS platforms add AI voice agent capabilities, CCaaS integrators may start competing for the same enterprise voice AI budget that sophisticated AAAs are beginning to target.
NO OVERLAP TODAY CONVERGING 2027+
🌍
Offshore BPO / "AI Upgrade" Shops
Traditional BPO providers (Philippines, India, Eastern Europe) pitching AI transformation to their existing enterprise clients.
Ecosystem Layer
Layer 4 · Legacy services transitioning to AI delivery · primarily operates at Layer 5 as buyer/operator
Primary Platforms Used
  • Existing CC platforms (Genesys, NICE)
  • Bolting on Google CCAI or AWS Connect
  • PolyAI or Replicant for voice AI
  • Rarely building custom; mostly integrating
Primary Buyers
  • Existing BPO enterprise clients
  • Companies seeking labor cost arbitrage
  • F500 and large mid-market
Revenue Model
  • Per-seat or per-hour BPO contracts
  • Transitioning to per-resolution pricing
  • Massive existing revenue at risk from AI
vs Agency Resellers
BPOs are actually being disrupted by the same voice AI that AAAs sell. Their "AI upgrade" pitch is defensive positioning to retain enterprise clients. No overlap with AAA SMB buyers. BPOs are more threat to Segment 8 platforms' enterprise direct sales than to AAAs themselves.
